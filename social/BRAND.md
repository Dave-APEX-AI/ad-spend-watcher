# CaillteAI — Brand Bible

## One line

**CaillteAI builds AI voice receptionists, automations, and smart websites that
make sure UK & Irish trades never lose another call, lead, or job.**

## The name does the heavy lifting

*Caillte* = "lost" in Irish. Every missed call is a lost job. Our entire content
universe hangs off the inversion of that word: **stop being caillte.** Use it as a
recurring motif ("Don't let leads go caillte", "Caillte = lost. CaillteAI = found").

## Category & positioning

- **Category:** AI voice receptionist + business automation for trades / home services.
- **Position:** Not "an AI agency". We are **the trades' AI receptionist** — built for
  plumbers, sparkies, solar, heating, drainage, windows, alarms, pumps. The newcomers
  are generalists chasing every vertical; we own one and speak its language.
- **Geography:** UK & Ireland. We sound local. We know it's a "job", not a "ticket";
  a "quote", not an "estimate request"; that the phone rings while you're up a ladder.
- **Proof, not hype:** the market is flooded with "AI agencies" popping up daily. We win
  by being *specific, useful, and relentless* — show the receptionist actually working,
  put real numbers on missed calls, and never post a faceless-guru platitude.

## Why we'll win (the wedge)

1. **Trade-native.** Demos use trade scenarios (emergency leak at 9pm, customer ringing
   three plumbers at once). Generic AI agencies can't fake this.
2. **The missed-call maths.** Our content is anchored in a number every owner feels:
   the £35k+/yr walking out the door in unanswered calls. That's our wedge and our CTA.
3. **Faceless & consistent.** No founder face to bottleneck output. The brand IS the
   character (see `series/office-heroes.md`). We can post daily, forever, on a £0 stack.
4. **Three products, one promise.** Receptionist (catch the call) → automation (book the
   job) → smart website (win the click). All ladder to "never go caillte".

## Tone of voice

- **Plain-spoken, confident, a bit cheeky.** Talk like a switched-on site foreman, not
  a SaaS landing page. Short sentences. No jargon ("LLM", "agentic", "conversational AI").
- **Show the pain, then the fix.** Lead with the lost job; land on the calm of never
  missing one.
- **Numbers over adjectives.** "£280 average job × 9 missed calls a week" beats "boost
  your revenue".
- **Irish/UK trade idiom welcome.** "Up a ladder", "on the tools", "ringing round",
  "cash job", "the missus does the books". Authentic > polished.
- **Never:** fearmongering for its own sake, fake urgency, generic motivational guru
  voice, emoji soup, "🚀 game-changer". One emoji max, used like punctuation.

### Voice cheat-sheet
| Don't say | Say |
|-----------|-----|
| "Leverage AI to optimise customer engagement" | "Your phone rang 11 times today. You answered 4." |
| "Our conversational AI solution" | "An AI receptionist that picks up every time" |
| "Drive ROI" | "That's 7 jobs you'll never know you lost" |
| "Book a discovery call" | "Find out what missed calls are costing you →" |

## Audience

Owner-operators and small directors of trades / home-services firms (1–50 staff) in the
UK & Ireland. They're on the tools or just off them, run lean, hate admin, lose calls
while working, and are sceptical of "tech". They scroll Instagram & Facebook reels in the
van, at lunch, and at 9pm on the sofa. They follow people like Joe Doyle, Tradify,
toolbox/banter pages, and trade suppliers.

## Visual identity (faceless system)

Designed so anyone can produce on-brand assets with no designer.

```
--ink:    #0A0F0D   /* near-black background / text */
--emerald:#0FB97E   /* primary brand green (nods to "Emerald" + Irish) */
--emerald-deep:#0A8F5F
--cream:  #F5F3EC   /* light backgrounds / text on ink */
--amber:  #FFC247   /* alert / "missed" / numbers that hurt */
--steel:  #8A938F   /* muted secondary text */
```

- **Logomark:** wordmark "CaillteAI" with the dot of the *i* as a green call-signal /
  soundwave. (If/when finalised, drop SVG in `social/brand-assets/`.)
- **Type:** bold geometric sans for headlines (e.g. Inter/Satoshi/Sora), heavy weight,
  tight tracking. Big numbers are a design element.
- **Motif:** the **green "answered" call dot** vs the **amber "missed" dot**. Soundwave
  bars. A van. A ringing phone. Keep it iconographic, never stock-photo-y.
- **Aspect ratios:** 1080×1350 (4:5 feed), 1080×1920 (reels/stories). Never square-only.
- **Captions on everything** — burned-in, big, mobile-first; assume sound-off.
