# Day 5 — Demo reel script: "I let an AI answer a plumber's phone"

Pillar 3 (Watch it work). The trust post — show, don't tell. 20–30s, 9:16, sound-on
rewarded but captioned for sound-off. Record on your Mac (QuickTime screen-rec + a real
or clearly-reconstructed call). **Label as a reconstruction if it's not a live call.**

## Shot list

| # | Time | On screen | Caption (burned-in) | Audio |
|---|------|-----------|---------------------|-------|
| 1 | 0:00–0:02 | Phone screen, incoming call, "11:02pm" | **"Turn the sound on — this isn't a person."** | ringtone |
| 2 | 0:02–0:06 | Call connects; waveform / CaillteAI UI | "11pm. The owner's asleep." | AI: *"Good evening, Murphy Plumbing — how can I help?"* |

| 6 | 0:23–0:27 | Owner's phone next morning: "1 job booked overnight" | "He woke up to a booked job." | — |
| 7 | 0:27–0:30 | End card: green soundwave | **"No missed call. No lost job. No night shift."** + "Free audit — link in bio" | — |

## Rules
- Real call = with consent; otherwise stamp **"Reconstruction"** in a corner. Redact any
  real names/addresses.
- Big captions, amber for the stakes (11pm), green for the wins (booked, deposit).
- First frame must read in 1.5s with sound off. No slow intro, no logo sting.
- Recut 9:16 → 4:5 for the feed. Mirror to Facebook.

## What you actually need to capture
1. A screen-recording of the AI booking flow (your product/dashboard), OR
2. A phone-call screen recording with the AI's voice, OR
3. A clean waveform animation over the captioned transcript (if you can't show the UI yet).

Any of the three works — the captions + the booking + the deposit are the proof.
