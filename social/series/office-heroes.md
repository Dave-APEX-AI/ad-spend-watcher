# Flagship Series — "Office Heroes" (CaillteAI cut)

A faceless, recurring content franchise — our version of Joe Doyle's *Office Heroes*,
re-pointed so it ladders straight to the product. This is the brand's repeatable world:
same characters, same beats, infinite episodes. Recurring format = recall = follows.

> **Working title:** **"Office Heroes"** — the unsung hero of every trade business is
> the one who *answers the phone*. Ours never sleeps. The twist: the hero is the AI.

## The core idea

Every trades business has the same silent villain — **the missed call** — and the same
fantasy hero — **someone who always picks up, books the job, and never has a bad day.**
That hero is CaillteAI. We personify it as a faceless brand character and run it through
recurring, snackable episode formats.

Because the angle (Q1) was left open, this is built as a **blend**: a faceless brand
character is the *spine*; **missed-call horror stories** are the primary recurring
*format*; and it pays off into the **free missed-call audit** CTA. Swap emphasis anytime.

## The cast (faceless, reusable)

- **CAILLTE** — the hero. The AI receptionist. We never show a face; it's represented as
  a calm green soundwave / glowing call-dot, a steady voice, captioned lines. Personality:
  unflappable, dry, quietly competent. Catchphrase: *"Already booked it."*
- **THE MISSED CALL** — the villain. An amber, buzzing, ignored phone. Rings out. Every
  ring = money leaving. Visualised as amber dots / a vibrating phone face-down in dust.
- **DAVE-ON-THE-TOOLS** — the everyman trade owner (not our Dave — *the* Dave; a stand-in
  for the viewer). Always up a ladder / under a sink / elbow-deep when the phone goes.
  Faceless: hi-vis sleeve, a hand, a van. The viewer projects themselves onto him.
- **THE COMPETITOR** — "the other three plumbers who *did* pick up." Off-screen menace.

## Episode formats (rotate these)

Each is 8–20s, sound-off-first, burned-in captions, one number, one payoff.

### 1. "The £___ Ring" (missed-call horror — primary format)
A single missed call dramatised as a lost job with a real number.
> *Cold open:* phone buzzing face-down in brick dust.
> *Caption beats:* "9:42pm. Burst pipe. She's panicking." → "You're asleep." →
> "She rings the next plumber." → "That call was worth £680." → "You'll never know it
> happened." → *CAILLTE green dot lights up:* "Unless someone picked up." → audit CTA.

### 2. "Already Booked It" (hero flex)
CAILLTE handles a call flawlessly while Dave's still up the ladder.
> Dave's phone rings → he can't answer → green dot pulses → "New job booked: Tue 9am,
> £420, deposit taken." → Dave climbs down to a booked job he did nothing for.
> Payoff line: *"Already booked it."*

### 3. "Caillte means lost" (brand-story micro)
15s explainer on the name. Education + memorability. High-save.
> "In Irish, *caillte* means lost." → "Every missed call is a lost job." →
> "We named the company after the thing we kill." → soundwave. → "CaillteAI."

### 4. "Villain Origin" (the missed call's POV)
Tongue-in-cheek: the Missed Call bragging about all the jobs it's stolen this week.
Banter pillar. Shareable. "11 missed calls this week. That's a holiday I funded for the
plumber down the road."

### 5. "Hero vs the other three" (competitor beat)
Customer rings 3 firms. Two ring out, one's "in a meeting", CAILLTE answers in 2 rings
and books it. "First to answer wins the job. Always has."

### 6. "Receipts" (proof / demo crossover)
Real (consented) or labelled-reconstruction call recording of CAILLTE booking a job,
captioned line-by-line. Bridges into `caillte-demo-clip`. Builds trust.

### 7. "Office Heroes Awards" (recurring segment)
Weekly tongue-in-cheek "award" — "This week's Office Hero: the AI that answered 47 calls
while Steve was on a roof in the rain." Builds the franchise ritual; great for a series
highlight + consistent posting hook.

## Recurring furniture (makes it a *franchise*, not random posts)

- **Cold open** always = a face-down buzzing phone OR a green dot waking up.
- **One number** per episode, big and amber when it's a loss, green when it's a win.
- **End card:** green soundwave + "Don't let leads go *caillte*." + soft audit CTA.
- **Catchphrase:** "Already booked it."
- **Series highlight** on the profile titled **Office Heroes** so new visitors binge it.
- **Naming:** caption every episode "Office Heroes — Ep. NN: <title>" for bingeability.

## Why this works (and is safe to "rip off")

Joe Doyle's *Office Heroes* is a *format/ritual*, not ownable IP — we're borrowing the
**recurring-character, celebrate-the-back-office** mechanic and pointing it at our
product. We don't copy his scripts, name, or likeness. Our hero is an AI; his is human
admin staff. Different enough to be ours, familiar enough to ride the proven mechanic.
